Learning About Animals: Bear, Lion and Shark

Bear

Bears are large mammals with thick fur and strong bodies, found in forests, mountains and Arctic regions. Most bears can walk on two legs for short periods and have an excellent sense of smell.

  • Weight: Usually between 100 and 600 kg, depending on the species.
  • Length: Around 1.7 to 2.8 meters from head to tail.
  • Main food sources: Berries, roots, insects, fish and small mammals (many species are omnivores).
  • Life expectancy: About 20–30 years in the wild.

Lion

Lions are powerful big cats that live mostly in African grasslands and savannas. They are known for their loud roar and for living in social groups called prides, where females usually do most of the hunting.

  • Weight: Adult males about 150–250 kg; females about 120–180 kg.
  • Length: About 2 to 3.3 meters including the tail.
  • Main food sources: Large herbivores such as zebras, antelopes and buffalo.
  • Life expectancy: Around 10–15 years in the wild.

Shark

Sharks are fish with a skeleton made of cartilage instead of bone. The great white shark is one of the best-known species, famous for its size and hunting skills in coastal and open ocean waters.

  • Weight: Many great white sharks weigh between 700 and 1,800 kg; the largest can reach about 2,500 kg.
  • Length: Usually around 4 to 5 meters, with some individuals over 6 meters.
  • Main food sources: Fish, seals, sea lions and other marine animals; they may also scavenge whale carcasses.
  • Life expectancy: Estimated up to about 70 years.
