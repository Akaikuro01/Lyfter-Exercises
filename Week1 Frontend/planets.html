Planets of the Solar System

Mars

Mars is a rocky planet often called the “Red Planet” because of its iron-rich soil. It has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system and is one of the main targets for future human exploration.

  • Approximate distance from the Sun: About 228 million km (1.52 AU)
  • Rotation period: About 24.6 hours (a little longer than an Earth day)
  • Number of moons: 2 (Phobos and Deimos)
  • Atmosphere: Thin atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and is classified as a gas giant. It has a strong magnetic field, dozens of moons and the famous Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has lasted for centuries.

  • Approximate distance from the Sun: About 778 million km (5.2 AU)
  • Rotation period: About 10 hours (very fast rotation)
  • Number of moons: More than 90 known moons
  • Atmosphere: Thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium

Saturn

Saturn is another gas giant, famous for its bright and wide ring system made of ice and rock. It is lighter than water in terms of average density and has many fascinating moons, including Titan.

  • Approximate distance from the Sun: About 1.43 billion km (9.5 AU)
  • Rotation period: About 10.7 hours
  • Number of moons: More than 80 known moons
  • Atmosphere: Thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium
